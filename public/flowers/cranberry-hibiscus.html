Description

Cranberry hibiscus plants are multi-stemmed shrubs that grow from 3 to 6 feet (1-2 m.) in height with green/red to burgundy serrated leaves. The foliage looks much like that of Japanese maple.


Planting requirements

Planting season: Year-round in Sri Lanka

Planting conditions:

Propagation Seeds
Planting method Plant the seeds in soil that stay moist and don't let the soil dry. Plant the soil in a depth of 0.5-1cm (0.25-0.5 inch) but cover lightly and don't push
Soil Grows in a variety of soil but requires well drained soil. Needs a pH balance of 6.0-7.0
Water Keep soil moist but not wet. Requires regular watering during dry seasons in Sri Lanka
Light Requires full sunshine but also grows in light shade

Growing conditions:

Temperatures Grows well in Sri Lankan temperatures ranging from 20-35°C
Soil Mix the soil with compost, humus or organic matter and mulch to cover the soil to keep it moist and weed free
Water Requires moderate levels of water. Increase watering during dry seasons
Pruning Prune in early dry season to encourage bushy growth
Weed control Mulch around the base of plants to suppress weeds and retain moisture.

Harvesting

Harvesting young full grown leaves cut next to the stems or with all the stems.


Curing

Hang the flowers upside down in a well-ventilated area until completely dry.


Storage

Store dried flowers in an airtight container away from light and heat. Leaves can be frozen for later use.


Protecting your plants

Pest control

Pest type: Aphids, mealybugs, root nematodes, and thrips

Symptoms: Curling leaves, yellowing leaves, sticky honeydew on leaves

Control method: Spray neem oil, introduce ladybugs as natural predators, use organic pesticides

Disease Control

Disease type: Leaf spot, root rot

Symptoms: Brown or black spots on leaves, wilting, yellowing leaves

Management: Avoid overwatering, ensure good drainage, remove infected parts, apply copper-based fungicides if necessary


cranberry Hibiscus

Generic name: Cranberry Hibiscus, African rosemallow
Sri Lankan name: Kuruluthuda
Scientific name: Hibiscus acetosella
Plant family: Malvaceae
Edible parts: Leaves and the Flower
Nutrition value: High in vitamin C, antioxidants, and minerals like calcium and iron.

Difficulty rating

Low country wet zone

Difficulty:

★★★☆☆

Explanation: Thrives in warm, humid conditions but requires attention to prevent fungal diseases
Challenges/Adaptations:

  • Ensure good drainage and air circulation to prevent fungal issues
  • Use raised beds or mounds in areas prone to waterlogging

Low country dry zone

Difficulty:

★★★☆☆

Explanation: Requires regular watering during prolonged dry periods but generally well-suited to the climate
Challenges/Adaptations:

  • Implement drip irrigation or water-conserving techniques
  • Use heavy mulching to retain soil moisture

Mid country

Difficulty:

★★☆☆☆

Explanation: Ideal growing conditions with moderate temperatures and rainfall
Challenges/Adaptations:

  • Monitor for pests during rainy seasons
  • Provide partial shade during exceptionally hot periods

Up country

Difficulty:

★★★★☆

Explanation: Can be grown but may require protection from cooler temperatures and strong winds
Challenges/Adaptations:

  • Use protective structures or grow near buildings for warmth
  • Choose sheltered locations to protect from strong winds
